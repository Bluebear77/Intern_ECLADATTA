Title: Unit prefixes

Value:
in both the   si   and isq  the prefix k stands for   kilokilo    meaning 1000  while ki is the symbol for the   binary prefix     kibi    meaning 1024  the binary prefixes were introduced in 1998 by the   international electrotechnical commission    iec  and in   ieee 15412002   which was reaffirmed 27 march 2008  the letter k is often used a nonstandard abbreviation for 1024  especially in  kb  to mean kib  the   kibibytekilobyte in its binary sense    in the context of data rates  however  typically only decimal prefixes are used  and they have their standard si interpretation
