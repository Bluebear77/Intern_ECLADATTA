Yearly summary

Australian Open series

Azarenka will begin her 2014 season as the second seed at the 2014 Brisbane International, in what will be her third participation at the event.[1] She reached the final for the first time since 2009, but lost to world number one Serena Williams in straight sets.[2]

Her next tournament following the Brisbane International was the Australian Open, where she was the two-time defending champion. She failed to defend her title, losing in the quarter-finals to Agnieszka Radwańska in three sets.[3]

Middle East series

Azarenka was due to play in the Qatar Total Open, where she was the two-time defending champion, but she withdrew before the tournament started due to a left foot injury.[4]

American hard court season

Azarenka returned from her foot injury at the BNP Paribas Open, but she lost her first match to Lauren Davis, marking her earliest ever exit from Indian Wells.[5] A recurrence of her foot injury at Indian Wells later forced her withdrawal from Miami for the second consecutive year.

Azarenka withdraws from the Albierto Monterry open for the second consecutive year.

Clay court season

Azarenka is scheduled to play at the Mutua Madrid Open, where she was upset in the second round by Ekaterina Makarova last year.[6]

She will also participate in Rome, where she was defeated in the final by Serena Williams last year.[7]

Azarenka will also participate at the French Open, where she reached the semi-finals last year.

Grass court season

Before Wimbledon Azarenka will play at the Eastbourne International.

Azarenka will have only 100 points to defend at Wimbledon, where she was one of many casualties from the tournament's third day last year.

US Open series

After Wimbledon Azarenka is scheduled to play in Stanford. Where she was winner in 2010.

Azarenka is scheduled to play the Rogers Cup, Cincinnati (where she is the defending champion) and the US Open, where she will be the defending finalist from the last two years.

Asian hard court season

Following the US Open, Azarenka will commence her Asian swing at the new Premier 5 tournament in Wuhan, and will also participate in Beijing, where she lost in the first round last year. Azarenka will only have 65 points to defend during this period.

WTA Tour Championships

Azarenka's qualification for the 2014 WTA Tour Championships will be dependent on how she performs this year.
