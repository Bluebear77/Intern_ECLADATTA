Tigerair Singapore currently flies to destinations within an approximate five-hour radius around Singapore to 35 destinations in nine countries around the region.[1] Thailand is its first and biggest market, with four cities served, taking advantage of the open-skies agreement between Singapore and Thailand. Current aviation agreements have prevented the airline from flying to neighbouring Malaysia except Kuala Lumpur (starting February 2008), although in September 2006, it was granted rights to fly to Kuching, Miri and Sibu in a bid to boost tourism in Sarawak.[2]
