Joya Ahsan (Bengali: জয়া আহসান, born Jaya Masud, July 1) is a Bangladeshi model and actress.

Early life and family

Ahsan is the eldest daughter of Late Md. A.S. Masud and Mrs. Rehana Masud. Her father was a freedom fighter during the Bangladesh war of liberation in 1971.[2][3]

Interested in singing and dancing, she took classes in music and acquired a diploma in the Bengali music genre Rabindra Sangeet.[4] She did her post-graduation studies from Eden University College, Dhaka.[5]

Ahsan first came to the telemedia when she performed in teledrama Panchami. While working on the drama she met one of the lead actors Golam Mustafa. Mustafa said she would do better if she worked more in the media. In the meantime she modeled for a calendar which caught the eye of Afzal Hossain, who later offered her work on a promotional advertisement of a soft drink.[6] Afterwards she left modeling and continued with her studies.

During that time she used to go the office of Bhorer Kagoj, a national daily of that time, rekindling her interest in working in the media. After a brief stint at a children's school she returned to modeling. There she met Faisal Ahsan who himself was a famous model, and they were soon married. Then she worked in Giashuddin Selim’s Shongshoy and it took her into the spotlight. According to her, Faisal had inspired her acting career a lot after their marriage.[7]

Initially, Ahsan's father was strictly against her ingress into the show business. However, he changed his views later.[8]

Career

Jaya Ahsan started her career as a model in 1990s in Bangladesh. She then started to work in television dramas, serials and later films.

Jaya worked in Giashuddin Selim’s Shongshoy. She portrayed various characters in teledramas such as Tevagha, Hatkura, Shahortolir Alo, Tarpor Paruler Deen, Tarporo Angurlata Nondoke, Bhalobashe and Labonnaprova,[2] as well as Anechy Shurjer Hashi.{{citation needed}}

On the big screen, Jaya was first seen in a special appearance in Bachelor, directed by Mostofa Sarwar Farooki. Jaya's first film was Nurul Alam Atik's debut film Dubshatar (English title: In Too Deep) (2010). She acted in Firey Esho Behula (2010). Though it was a big budget movie, it was not a commercial success. In 2010-2011 she worked in Nasiruddin Yousuff’s film Guerrilla which was released on April 14, 2011 in Bangladesh. {{citation needed}}

In the following year Jaya worked on Chorabali which is an action thriller released in 2012. The film is written and directed by Redoan Rony.[9]

Jaya has also performed in the Bangla film Aborto in 2013.[10] Ahsan performed in Purno Doirgho Prem Kahini (Full Length Love Story) a Bangladeshi romantic film directed by Arindam Sil. This is the first collaboration between Shakib Khan and Ahsan..{{citation needed}} The film was released on 16 October 2013 on the occasion of Eid Al-Adha.

Jaya is now working in Samurai Maruf's action / science-fiction film Parle Theka which will present her as an action heroine.[11][unreliable source?]

Awards

Ahsan received The National Film Award of Bangladesh two times.[21] Ahsan received her first National Film Award for her performance in the film Guerrilla. She won best actress award at the 11th Dhaka International Film Award 2012.[22][23]

She Won the Best Actress Award in National Film Awards 2013 the second time for her performance in Chorabali.[24]

Ahsan has received numerous Awards and International Recognition, and has established herself as one of Bangladesh's most high-profile celebrities.
